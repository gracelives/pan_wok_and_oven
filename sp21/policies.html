Policies

Course Description

This course aims to build a bridge between the Data 8 curriculum and hands-on data science. Intended for students who have just completed Data 8 and plan to study fields that involve working with data, the course will translate knowledge of the datascience package to the pandas library, and give students supplemental technical skills to support their own data analysis projects. Data science methods such as finding robust datasets, dealing with missing values, and assessing the validity of the analysis will be taught. By the end of the semester, students will have carried out a start-to-finish analysis of data of their choosing. Upon completing this course, students will be able to use industry-grade data science tools to analyze data for projects in all fields.

Prerequisites

Students should have completed Data 8 before taking this course. It is also recommended, but not required, that students have taken a linear algebra course such as Math 54.

Lectures

Lectures will consist of two segments: a pre-recorded set of youtube videos to be watched at home with a follow-along Jupyter Notebook, and a live, synchronous lecture. They are designed to work hand in hand and one is not a substitute for the other. Live lectures will be during the scheduled class time of Mondays from 5:00 - 6:00 PM PST, and will occasionally feature guest lecturers from the data science industry. They will NOT be recorded and attendance will be taken.

Participation

The class will be run as much like a seminar as a regular class. Your participation is necessary to make this work. We will be expecting you to discuss during class, participate on Piazza, and come to Office Hours. You are required to check in with the course staff team for at least once during the semester. We also need your feedback on our materials in order to improve them!

If you are unable to attend class, email the instructor to have your absence excused 24 hours before the lecture. Students are allowed 2 excused absences during the semester. If you are unable to attend lecture without notice, please post privately on Piazza so that the course staff are aware.

Participation will be graded based on attendance and overall interaction with other students and course staff. As mentioned above, attendance will be taken during lecture, and students will be awarded full participation credit for attending at least 7/12 of live lectures and checking in with a course staff member before and after the final project. This will be worth 20% of the final course grade.

Labs

Labs are designed to help you gain familiarity and practice the new concepts taught in lecture. They are to be completed individually and then reviewed together as a group. There will be three labs throughout the course, and each will be worth 10% of the final course grade.

Final Project

The final project will be a culmination of everything taught in the class, going through the entire data science lifecycle. It will be worth 50% of the final course grade.

For more information on the project, check out the prompt & rubric and example datasets & projects under the Resources tab of the navigation bar.

Grades

Grades will be assigned using the following weighted components:

Activity Grade
Participation 20%
Labs 30%
Project 50%

This class will only be offered for P/NP credit. To earn a passing grade, students must earn at least 70% of the total points offered.
